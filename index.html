About Me

I am a machine learning enthusiast, in the second year of my PhD at UiT Tromsø, currently working on Explainable AI as a PhD Candidate with Dr. Michael Kampffmeyer, Dr. Robert Jenssen and Dr. Marina Marie-Claire Höhne. My future work as a PhD candidate will focus on developing explainable AI algorithms with limited labels, i.e, focusing on Domain Adaptation/Few-Shot Learning/Self-Supervised Learning. With the help of previous research opportunities, I have an extensive exposure of 4 years in medical image analysis. Considering this, I also plan to extend all my current and future work for the medical domain where learning with limited labels, as well as transparency in terms of explainable AI, is of the utmost importance.

Previously, I completed my research-focussed masters in Medical Image Analysis from IIT, working on Automated Cervical Cancer Detection with Dr. Arnav Bhavsar, Dr. Anil K. Sao and Aindra Systems. We were able to develop a low cost full scale cervival cancer screening device as a satisfactory byproduct of this research. Afterwards, I went on to work for AI and healthcare based companies (Arkray, Inc) before starting a PhD in Norway. With Arkray, we developed a urine sediment analysis device, currently in the market (AUTION EYE AI-4510). I am ready to take on challenging machine learning research problems, especially when directly or indirectly related to contributions in the medical domain. I am at heart a computer science graduate, with parallel interests in web designing, sketching, painting, music and books.
